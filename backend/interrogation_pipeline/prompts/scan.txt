STEP 1 — MANDATORY HOMICIDE CHECK (the ONLY rejection criterion)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACCEPT if the case involves a death caused by another person, including:
- murder, attempted murder, manslaughter, homicide
- vehicular homicide / DUI causing death
- capital murder, felony murder
- fatal shooting, fatal stabbing, fatal beating
- fatal child abuse, fatal neglect, child death at hands of caregiver
- death during commission of another crime (robbery-homicide, etc.)
- suspicious death being investigated as homicide
- "killed", "deceased victim", "pronounced dead at scene", "died from
  injuries" — implicit references count if context is clearly criminal

REJECT ONLY if there is NO death involved, e.g.:
- theft, robbery, fraud (no death)
- DUI / traffic stop with no fatality
- assault, domestic dispute with no fatality
- drug possession, weapons charges alone
- mental health calls, welfare checks with no death
- civil disputes, trespassing

DO NOT reject for missing interrogation footage. Bodycam-only cases are
ACCEPTED. The presence/absence of interrogation only affects categorization
in Step 2, never rejection.

If you are uncertain whether someone died, lean toward ACCEPT and flag
it as "interrogation_unsure".
